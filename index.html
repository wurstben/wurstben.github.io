About Me

Overview
My multidisciplinary background in statistics, biology, and business analytics provide a wide variety of skills and techniques that I can utilize when researching and developing solutions to quantitative problems. The focus of my expertise is with data; from collection to reporting results to businesses and researchers, I have gone through all the steps necessary to analyze and create valuable results from many projects and professional settings. With interest in science, technology, business, and economics, I am highly motivated in many fields to seek out data-driven insights in a variety of industries and roles in data analytics or research and development.

Education
I completed my undergrad at Michigan State University, where I started with marine biology in mind. I became very interested in data, and thus pursued statistics as a second degree. As I grew more familiar with the role mathematics plays in both science and statistics, I added minors in both mathematics and computational mathematics, Science, and Engineering (CMSE). Towards the end of my undergrad, I became interested in technology and its role in data science. I also had a personal interest in business, so I went back to Michigan State University (specifically the Broad College of Business) for a Master’s in Business Analytics. This background gives be a lot of experience in science, working with data, and problem solving in a multidisciplinary setting.

Research Experience
Starting as an undergraduate researcher, I was introduced to academia at Boughman Laboratory where I worked under Dr. Jannette Boughman on various projects involving three-spine stickleback fish. During my time there, I gained valuable experience in experimental design, data analysis, and evolutionary study. I was able to present several projects for research symposiums and was an author on research headed by Dr. Jason Keagy published in Evolutionary Ecology Research called “The relative roles of genes and rearing environment on the spatial cognitive ability of two sympatric species of threespine stickleback”. In this position, I was also awarded the Outstanding Academic Achievement and Promise in Zoology Award by Michigan State University College of Natural Science Integrative biology department.
Towards the end of my research experience, I was promoted to the role of Laboratory Technician where I oversaw the care of live research specimens and helped manage data storage for multiple projects. I helped budget and assemble specialized computer equipment for the use in scientific research. Lastly, I assisted in the renovation and organization of the laboratory space.

Corporate Experience
At the Ford Motor Company GDI&A department, I was tasked with building several key performance indicators (KPIs) from a large dataset related to product usage provided by business partners. By carefully studying past workflows and running exploratory data analysis, I was able to generate both the requested conclusions and emphasize anomalies for further investigation. Implementation of Long Short-Term Memory Layers Using COVID-19 and Population Data


Summary
The SEIR/SEIRS model has been the focus of numerous studies related to COVID-19’s outbreak in 2020. This objective of the study is to utilize the population structure and design of previous studies with Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) neural network architecture to improve the capture of key parameters using the United States as a case study. The resulting model shows promise but low practicability, and the key outcomes indicate that more flexible designs and further experimentation into LSTM models can lead to better predictions of variables essential to understanding viral outbreaks in populations.

Skills Used
Python, TensorFlow, Data Mining, Neural Networks, LSTM, Biology

Sentiment Analysis of Tweets Related to Tech Launch Events and Comparisons with Stock Price Trends


Summary
The team’s objective is to use Twitter and stock data to investigate how these announcements truly impact potential consumer opinions of a product. We used a variety of data mining techniques to extract Tweets related to these events and analyze the change of sentiment before and after an event as a measure of announcement performance and summarize announcement reactions from the public. First, we investigated whether or not perception changed immediately after an announcement by comparing the sentiment of tweets before and after a given event. Second, we measured the impact of announcement reactions on a company's value in the stock market. This was accomplished by comparing the change in sentiment before/after an event and the change in stock price on that day. By directly comparing the sentiment to stock prices, we were able to quantify the success of each event. Our analysis focused on the following five events: Apple Fall 2019, Apple Fall 2020, Nvidia 1000 Series (2016), Nvidia 2000 Series (2018), and the Nvidia 3000 Series (2020).

Skills Used Python, Twitter API, Data Mining, Sentiment Analysis, Linear Regression

Investigating Factors of Popular Music Using Spotify API


Summary
The team set out to look at the data involving popular music on Spotify using their built in API. The objective was to look at differences between popular and non-popular songs to look for patterns and key factors that might help predict which songs hit the top of the charts. From our analysis, we found that depending on the factors considered, there are some attributes like dance, beats per minute, and loudness were significant predictors, but they were not helpful in explaining variance (low R-square value).

Skills Used

A Sentiment Analysis of Political Articles from The New York Times (2015-2018)


Summary
The importance of an open and free press is paramount to sharing of new ideas, but comes at the cost of bias, especially given the ease of access many people have to disreputable sources. There is a value in opinion pieces, especially from highly intelligent individuals, who possess significantly valuable visions about their field of expertise. Many people have experts they trust to discuss their favorite topic. This project sets out to do two things related to opinion-centric articles, especially on the political topic. Firstly, it uses language processing techniques to categorize articles as political or not given the content, and secondly, tracks changes of the sentiment over time within political content.
While categorizing was not successful (likely due to overlapping characteristics between topics), we did find a significant trend in sentiment of political articles by year. The more recent the article, the more positive the sentiment tends to be.

Skills Used
Python, Data Management, Data Mining, Tableau, Linear Regression, Machine Learning, DBSCAN, Sentiment Analysis

Project 7: Football Revenue


Summary
Every time a college football game is played, bragging rights and school pride are on the line. Players, coaches, and fans sacrifice time, money, and energy every Saturday to win. This study investigates the off-field effect of those winning programs and how performance affects team revenues, local economies, and undergraduate application volume for the school. By combining attributes from various data sources complete data sets were created. Using these data sets, a number of measures for college football teams and local economies were identified as variables in linear regression analysis. These tools and techniques were utilized to determine if certain variables (such as team success, defined in later sections) are significant in predicting team revenue, economic growth, or application volume. The resulting models found some metrics of team performance, attendance, and university applications significant in predicting team revenue and local changes in GDP. Similarly, bowl game performance has an effect on application volume. However, some models suffer from issues such as heteroskedasticity and non-normality.

Skills Used Linear regression, Data Mining, Python, R

Regression Techniques to Investigate COVID-19 Outbreak (May 2020)


Summary
The team used linear and logistic methods to attempt to predict increases in cases and deaths due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The data was gathered from reputable online sources such as Johns Hopkins. The project found some trivial trends (such as case increases predict death increases), but limitations in the scope of the data (first 2-3 months of the outbreak) reduced the result significant. There were other factors that were not addressed such as not all locations had the same restrictions.

Skills Used
R, Data Mining, Linear Regression, Logistic Regression
